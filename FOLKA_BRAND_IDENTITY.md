# Folka Coffee Solutions — Brand Identity & Business Context
 
## About
 
**Folka Coffee Solutions** (also branded as **CAFE FOLKA** on their e-commerce) is a specialty coffee company born between **Monterrey, Mexico** and **San Antonio, Texas**. The brand was created to bridge the gap between coffee producers, passionate cafés, and curious drinkers.
 
Folka is more than a coffee retailer — their core mission is to **help independent coffee shops and cafés thrive** by providing equipment, products, consulting, training, and real support.

## Color Palette
 
| Name           | Role             | Hex (approx.) | Usage                                      |
|----------------|------------------|---------------|---------------------------------------------|
| Midnight Blue  | Primary / Dark   | `#101C2E`     | Dark backgrounds, primary logo color, text  |
| Desert White   | Light / Neutral  | `#F2EDE3`     | Light backgrounds, paper, cards             |
| Mineral Sand   | Accent / Warm    | `#B5A88A`     | Accent backgrounds, secondary elements      |
 
### Color rules
- High contrast between text and background always.
- Dark backgrounds (Midnight Blue) → Desert White or Mineral Sand for text/logos.
- Light backgrounds (Desert White) → Midnight Blue for text/logos.
- Mineral Sand backgrounds → Midnight Blue for text/logos.
- Never use Mineral Sand text on Desert White (low contrast).

## Typography
 
- **Primary typeface:** Custom geometric sans-serif, all-caps, wide letter-spacing.
- The **"L"** in FOLKA has a distinctive flat/cut top — unique visual signature.
- **"COFFEE SOLUTIONS"** subtitle uses a lighter weight or slightly condensed italic variant.

## NEW CREATIVE DIRECTION (2026)
 
> **IMPORTANT:** The current site (folkasolutions.com) uses a standard Shopify catalog approach with clean white backgrounds. The new direction moves toward a premium editorial/magazine aesthetic. All new content, pages, and designs should follow this new direction, NOT the current site style.
 
### Visual References & Inspiration
 
| Brand | What to take from it |
|-------|---------------------|
| **Smeg** (smeg.com) | Product as design hero. Aspirational lifestyle photography. Clean layouts with generous white space. Bold typography. "Collections" and "Inspiration" sections instead of flat catalog grids. The product IS the visual — no clutter around it. |
| **La Marzocco Home** | Dramatic product photography with moody lighting. Dark backgrounds with accent colors (deep reds, warm metals). Cinematic feel — machines presented like industrial art pieces. Close-up detail shots of chrome, steam, pressure gauges. |
| **Origin Coffee Roasters** | Warm editorial storytelling. Lifestyle photography of baristas at work, cafés, origins. "Trusted by your favourite baristas" — social proof woven into the visual language. Earthy, natural tones. |
| **xBloom** | Modern DTC approach. Reviews/ratings integrated into hero visuals. Clean product cards with lifestyle context. |
 
### Editorial Photography Principles
 
**Product photography (new standard):**
- Hero shots: Single product, dramatic lighting, minimal or dark background
- Detail shots: Extreme close-ups of textures, chrome, dials, portafilters
- Lifestyle shots: Product in context of a beautiful home bar or café setup
- NO flat catalog white-background shots for hero/feature content
- Moody, cinematic lighting with directional light sources
- Earth tones from the Folka palette as backgrounds (Midnight Blue, warm grays)
 
**Content photography:**
- Baristas at work — real people, real cafés
- Coffee preparation process — pouring, extracting, steaming
- Origin/farm photography for coffee storytelling
- Behind-the-scenes at the Folka roastery and showroom
- B&W editorial photography for premium equipment features (like the Rocket Cinquantotto series already produced)
 
### Layout & Design Principles
 
- **Generous white space** — let the product breathe
- **Large-scale hero images** — full-bleed, high-impact product photography
- **Editorial grid layouts** — magazine-style content blocks, NOT uniform product grids
- **Bold, oversized typography** — section headers as design elements
- **Scroll-based storytelling** — long-form product pages with progressive disclosure
- **Collections as editorial stories** — each brand/category gets a curated editorial presentation, not just a product list
- **Minimal UI chrome** — navigation and controls should be understated
 
### Web Page Structure (Smeg-inspired)
 
Instead of the current catalog approach, pages should follow this hierarchy:
1. **Hero banner** — Full-width cinematic product image with minimal text overlay
2. **Brand story / Editorial intro** — Short, compelling narrative about the collection
3. **Featured products** — Large editorial cards (not small grid thumbnails)
4. **Detail/Lifestyle gallery** — Close-up shots, in-context photography
5. **Specs & Features** — Clean, scannable technical information
6. **Related content** — Blog posts, brew guides, barista stories

## CONTENT STRATEGY — "Ecosistema Folka"
 
> Based on the internal content strategy document. This defines how Folka creates and positions content.
 
### Strategic Goals
 
1. **Reposicionar la comunicación de la marca** — Move from a product-catalog approach to a specialist coffee brand where content transmits knowledge, culture, and experience.
2. **Generar percepción de expertise** — Position Folka as the authority in coffee and equipment, showing deep knowledge of categories, processes, and real product use cases.
3. **Humanizar la marca** — Integrate the team, baristas, and real product usage to project authentic experience and connect with the coffee community.
 
### Two Content Pillars
 
#### Pillar 1: "Detrás de una buena taza hay un sistema completo"
- **Insight:** Most people think only the machine matters, but the result depends on the grinder, calibration, water, technique, and equipment working together.
- **Content opportunities:**
  - The professional coffee ecosystem (how all pieces connect)
  - How to build a professional coffee bar
  - Essential tools and why they matter
- **Goal:** Position Folka as an integral provider, not just a product seller.
 
#### Pillar 2: "Los profesionales confían en herramientas profesionales"
- **Insight:** Baristas and specialty cafés don't improvise their equipment. They carefully select tools that support their craft.
- **Content opportunities:**
  - Barista interviews and profiles
  - Café stories and case studies
  - "Why we chose this machine" — real testimonials from working cafés
- **Goal:** Build authority and credibility through real-world social proof.
 
### Content Types
 
- **Product editorials** — Deep-dive features on specific machines/grinders (like the Rocket Cinquantotto and Slayer Steam Single posts already produced)
- **Brew guides** — Step-by-step brewing methods with the Folka perspective
- **Café spotlights** — Stories of independent cafés Folka works with
- **Barista profiles** — Interviews highlighting craft and expertise
- **Equipment comparisons** — Educational content helping buyers make informed decisions
- **Behind the scenes** — Roastery tours, showroom visits, team stories
- **Origin stories** — Coffee sourcing from Chiapas and Guerrero
 
### Tone in Content
 
- Speak as a knowledgeable peer, not a salesperson
- Lead with the "why" before the "what" — explain the purpose of a tool before listing specs
- Use storytelling to make equipment feel human and purposeful
- Mix Spanish and English naturally — the audience is bilingual
- Avoid generic marketing language ("best in class", "world-class", "premium quality")
